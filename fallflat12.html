Human Fall Flat Level 12 - Ice

Level 12 - Ice

 

Text Walkthrough:

Our next update level is Ice! This is another lengthy level that has its frustrating moments, and unfortunately, the achievements here can be somewhat glitchy, especially the one involving the ski jump, so be prepared as you might have some difficulty getting them to unlock. If you've made it through everything before this, though, then persevere as you can definitely get through this without too many problems and it's the only level you have left!

Immediately as you drop into the level, you'll see two ice blocks directly in front of you. Grab the one on the right and drag it along the shadows on the ground up to the wall behind you so you can climb up onto the ice cube and onto the wall. Walk along the log platform and through the gap into a small area with two more ice blocks. Drag one of them out of the shadows into the light patch to the right. This will cause it to start to melt. Before it melts completely, drag it back into the shadows so you have a much smaller ice cube. Stand it up flat against the wall. It should now be able to fit through the gap. Pull it through the gap and around the corner back towards the start, keeping it close to the wall so it remains in the shadows. Stand it up against the log wall and climb up on top of the ice cube, then up onto the wall into the next area.

Head down the path here and straight ahead after exiting the small tunnel. Climb up the ledges to get on top of the overhang. Grab one of the wooden boards laying against the cliff wall up here, then walk to the left along the log towards the little wooden roof. Place the board here so it hangs off the metal grate towards the log platform with the red X sign on it. Go back to grab another wooden board and bring it to this spot again, dropping it on top of the other wooden board away from the edge that is jutting out so that it pins down that board. You can then safely jump off the end of that board and grab onto the platform ahead.

Follow the path past more signs then jump from the end and grab onto the giant icicle ahead. It will drop down into the ground below. Turn around and climb up the little wooden ledge behind you to get back up to where you were, then jump onto the top of the icicle and climb up the various ledges ahead. You will end up at the top of a clearing with some houses; jump down to the left to land on the icy ramp and head down.

You will see two ice cubes and a snowman ahead of you, but for the first achievement in this level, you must complete the following seesaw puzzle without using the ice cubes, so skate right past those and grab the head off the snowman instead (you monster!). Drag the head around to the right, following the shadows around until you get to the biggest shadowy patch near the ramp we just came down. Roll the head around here and it will increase in size as snow gets added to it. You want to get it so that it’s taller than your character at least. Now grab the floating platform nearby and pull it down to the ground by the snowball, making sure it's inside the shadows, then get on the platform yourself and pick up the snowball to put it on there with you. Aim the camera up to lift the snowball slightly off the ground if you’re struggling to get it on the platform. Now you want to try to climb on top of the snowball and on top of the seesaw contraption from there. It's a bit tricky, but nothing compared to some of the climbs from earlier in the game. Once you’re safely on top of the seesaw, walk straight along it and jump off the end onto the ledge, then head over to the top of the snowboarding hill. You'll unlock:

 

No Ice Ice Baby

Complete the seesaw puzzle in "Ice" without using an ice cube

 
4 guides

 

(I should note that when I unlocked this achievement initially, I used a different method where I climbed from the ice cubes up to the other platform, then up onto the ledge from there, and it would not unlock, despite this method being proven to work for other people. After repeating this method several times and still not unlocking it, I then went to the checkpoint after the ski jump, quit out from the level, reloaded back in and walked back up the hill to the ledge. After walking around a bit, I restarted the level entirely and the achievement unlocked. It's possible that method no longer works due to a patch and I managed to unlock it through a glitch, but considering how broken the next achievement is, this one could just be broken as well. The method I described in the previous paragraph is one that I used on a second account, and it unlocked first try for me, but in the event that you find the achievement won’t unlock for you this way, try the steps from this paragraph to see if it will do the trick.)

Immediately after that, you have the opportunity for another achievement, but it’s one that’s unfortunately very glitchy and it can be a pain to unlock. I’ll go through how you are supposed to get the achievement first, and then I'll list some things to try if you can’t get the achievement to unlock. What you’re meant to do is go down this hill and complete the jump with two snowboards strapped to your feet. Grab a snowboard, place it flat on the ground and carefully get one of your feet to step on either of the black straps on top of the snowboard. It will get stuck to the snowboard. If you end up getting both feet stuck to it, reach down and grab the snowboard, then move it around and it should become unstuck from your feet. Now get another snowboard and repeat this with your other foot. With two snowboards strapped to your feet, head to either side of the slope. It’s best not to go straight down the middle as it’s hard to steer and you’ll likely end up crashing into one of the trees. Once you’re ready, head down the slope and with some luck, you’ll launch off the jump at the bottom and fly safely over the gap. At that point, you should unlock:

 

Tricky

Complete the slope jump in "Ice" with 2 snowboards strapped to your feet

 
2 guides

 

Now there’s a good chance that you’ll do everything exactly right and you find the achievement won't unlock. It doesn't necessarily mean you've done it wrong, as there’s plenty of reports of people saying that they’ve landed the jump successfully several times but not getting the achievement. However, people have also not made the jump or done it in some other weird way and have had the achievement unlock for them. If it doesn't unlock for you the proper way, here are some things you can try:

  • Try intentionally messing up the jump and falling into the gap or stopping short of the jump entirely. Some people have reported this working for them.
  • Replay the level again and avoid reloading any checkpoint up until you’ve done the jump. There's a chance that reloading from a checkpoint could break the achievement in some way.
  • If you make the jump and it doesn’t unlock, you can try and jump back over the gap, climb up to the top of the hill again and repeat the jump once more with a different snowboard. Potentially the trigger for the achievement unlocking doesn’t always work properly on the first jump, but a second one will hopefully do the trick. This is how I got it to unlock on my second account after making the jump successfully on my first attempt but not getting the achievement.
  • And just to be thorough, I'll say how I got it on my first account: I went down the hill with just one snowboard strapped to one of my feet and I got stuck at the bottom of the hill and ended up falling into the jump, and the achievement unlocked as I was falling down. Yep… a far more apt name for this achievement would be Glitchy. I suppose if nothing else works, you can try just using one snowboard on one of your feet to see if that will unlock it.

Let’s say you’ve got the achievement or want to move on and try it later. If you’re on the other side of the jump, great. If you’re up at the top of the hill, get both of your feet onto one snowboard and glide down the ramp. You should easily make it over the jump this way and hey, who knows, you might unlock the achievement this way if you don’t already have it. Once you’re over here, grab the snowboard if it’s still attached to you and move it about to get it unstuck from your feet, then jump on top of the circular wooden platform with the screw in the middle. While facing the wall ahead of you, grab the handle on the left side, and walk forward. The platform should slowly start to rise. When it’s up high enough for you to reach the top of the wall, let go and quickly jump over on top of it.

You’ll see three boxes up here containing gray blocks that are being held up by ropes. For clarity’s sake, when I say box, I am referring to these rather than the wooden crates. Jump onto the middle box so it sinks down into the ground. Grab the nearby crate and quickly place it onto the box so that it stays weighed down in the ground. Go left and climb up onto the stack of blocks, then onto the tall box next onto it and quickly jump onto the wall past it before the box sinks too far. Head into the house to your right and grab the crate inside. Drag it outside and by the box at the front of the house. Jump onto that box so it sinks down, and again grab the crate and place it on top of the box to keep it weighed down. Now climb onto the stack of blocks by the other box, then up onto the box itself, and quickly climb up onto the roof ahead.

Grab the large wooden board up here and fall back down with it. Drop it on the ground and look to the lower area. What you want to do is carefully use the wooden board to create a ramp from here down to the top of either of the stacks of blocks below. Line it up with either stack of blocks as you carefully push it off the edge and get it to rest on the stack below creating yourself a ramp. Now drop down to that area and get the crate from on top of the middle box. Drag it over to the right box, jump on top of it and drag the crate onto it as before. Now use your makeshift ramp to get back up to the upper area, carefully jumping up along the middle of the ramp so it doesn’t tip to either side. Grab the crate from the right box up here and take it to the left box. Jump onto the box and drag the crate on top. Now get on top of the stack of blocks by the right box, climb up onto it and quickly jump onto the roof of the house. Climb onto the stack of blocks up here, onto one final box, and up onto the ledge in the next area.

You’ll see some logs stacked up against each other in a triangular shape on the left. Grab any one of them and place them in between the posts on both sides of the gap up ahead to create a bridge. Grab another one of the logs and place it by the other one across the gap, then grab a third log and jump across the logs to the next gap. Place this log in the gap in a similar fashion, then grab one of the logs from the previous gap and place it in the second gap also before heading across the logs. Alternatively, if you feel more confident in your jumping ability, you can jump across the first log with the second log in hand, then place that log over the second gap and jump straight across it. Now climb up to the stone wall on your right and look straight down as you climb up to ensure you don’t go flying over the wall. Turn to face the stacked stone pillar to your right, then jump and grab the top of it so that it falls apart. Grab the smallest stone you can and jump back across the bridge with the stone in hand. Be careful, especially if you used the alternative method and only have one log to jump across. Head to the ice doorway slightly to the left and smack the stone against it. After a few good hits, it will break open. Drop the stone and head into the cave.

Grab the large wooden plank propped up against the structure to the left and use it to create a ramp up to the platform on the right with the fire. Get it laid as flat and smooth as possible. Next, pull the sled with crates tied to it up against the structure so you can climb up the crates and onto the lower roof. Climb up to the higher roof and Grab the ice block with the stick frozen inside it, then drag it back down to the ground with you. Go towards the ramp, turn your back to the ramp and walk backwards up the middle of it whilst dragging the block up. You should be able to get it up to the fire this way. Once the fire has melted the block, grab the stick, set it on fire and use it to melt the ice encasing the lever near the wooden gate. Pull that lever and head through to the next area.

We will set up our final miscellaneous achievement in this last area, and thankfully it doesn’t appear to be anywhere near as glitchy as the other achievements. Drop straight down through the gate and stand on the button. It will cause the ski lifts to start moving. Wait until the chair behind you comes around to the other side of the ski lift support, then step off the button so it stops and go behind you to the wooden pillars with red tarps on top. Basically, you want to move each of these pillars around so that the tarps create a shadowy path along the ground all the way back to the button. This way, you can grab the ice block by the house past the pillars and drag it through the shadows back to the button. It doesn’t have to be a perfect path, but you need the ice block to remain mostly intact by the time you get it to the button. Once you’ve got it there, drag the ice block on top of the button, then quickly run to the nearest ski lift and climb on. Once you’re up into a ski lift, ride it up to above the ice wall with the box on top and drop down on top of it. If you mess this up, don’t worry; the ice block will respawn back by the house and you can stand on the button to move the ski lift into a good position for the next attempt.

Once you’re up on top of the wall, jump to the crate in front of you, then turn around and head left to find another crate that is secluded on its own platform. Jump over to this crate and push it into the ditch in the middle. Move it far enough forwards so that you can use it as a bridge to push the other crate onto it and across the gap. Once you’re across with it, simply take it back down to the main area and place it onto the button so the ski lifts are running again. Now grab onto a lift when it comes by and let it take you around one complete revolution to the top and back down again. Once you've done this, you'll unlock:

 

Taking the Piste

Complete 1 complete revolution on the ski chairs in "Ice"

 
2 guides

 

It did take about two and a half revolutions for me, but I was hanging from the bottom of the chair for a while before I could climb up which might have caused it not to register that I was in the chair.

Anyways, once the achievement pops, stay on the lift until you reach the very top of this area where you will see a platform with sticks of dynamite and a plunger. Push the plunger to detonate the giant statue on the far mountain which will topple down and create a path for you. The way forwards from here is relatively self-explanatory; just take your time and carefully climb up each ledge and platform. At the far end is a cave and a giant hole which is our exit. Drop in and you’re finished!

Well, not quite. You'll be dropped into a snowy version of the very first level, Mansion. Quickly make your way through the level which will be trivial to you now, drop through the exit, and the level is now truly finished. As the credits roll, you'll unlock:

 

Avalanche

Complete "Ice"

 
2 guides

 

Now I am pretty certain that the next levels, Thermal and Factory, are not considered part of the main game, as even with them installed, completing Ice again took me into the credits, so if you managed to beat every level but that one in a single sitting, at this point, you should unlock:

 

Speedrun

Complete the game start-to-end in one run

 
7 guides

 

It took a couple of seconds for the Speedrun achievement to unlock for me, and it didn't pop at the same time as the Avalanche achievement, so don't worry if it doesn't unlock straight away. If you didn't do all the previous levels in one sitting, you'll now have to replay everything up to Ice again without quitting, but it should go much quicker this time as you'll know where you're going, you'll have a good grasp of the controls and you can skip anything relating to other achievements unless you happen to have missed any of them. I estimate it will take about 2-3 hours if you run straight through everything at a decent pace.

And that's another level finished! If you've played through the whole game up to this point, you will likely also have unlocked the Adventurer achievement for travelling 25 km. I unlocked it not long before the end of the Ice level on my first playthrough of the game, but that was with a replay of the Aztec level for the two miscellaneous achievements. If you still have the Speedrun achievement to unlock, then you will unlock Adventurer for sure on your second playthrough for that achievement. If you happen to get the Speedrun achievement on your first playthrough and still don't have Adventurer, you shouldn't have much more further to go for the achievement and you can just quickly replay through the first few levels of the game, or the next few update levels, to get the last bit of distance. Once you have travelled 25 km, you'll unlock:

 

Adventurer

Travel 25km
